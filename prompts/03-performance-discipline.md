# Prompt 3 — Performance & Discipline

## Use This For
Generating performance management and disciplinary policies. This is the hardest section to write because it needs to be fair, enforceable, and legally defensible. The PIP process here costs $300–600 from HR consultants alone.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an HR policy writer creating legally-aware performance and discipline policies. Be direct and specific — vague policies get ignored and create liability.

**Company:** [Company Name]
**State:** [State — at-will language varies]
**Review cycle:** [Annual / Semi-annual / Quarterly / 90-day new hire + annual]
**Review format:** [Manager-only / Self + manager / 360-degree]
**Discipline philosophy:** [Progressive discipline (verbal → written → PIP → termination) / Performance-based only / Immediate termination for serious violations]
**At-will state:** [Yes/No — most US states are at-will; California and Montana have nuances]
**Reference policy:** [Will you provide employment references? Yes (title and dates only) / No references / Full references at manager discretion]

Generate these policies:

---

### POLICY 10: PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS

Include:
- Review cycle and timing
- Who participates (manager, employee self-review, peers if 360)
- What is evaluated (core competencies, goals, culture fit, growth)
- How ratings work (scale description, what each level means)
- How reviews connect to compensation decisions
- New hire 90-day check-in (separate from annual cycle)
- Manager preparation requirements (gather feedback at least X weeks before)
- Employee right to respond to written review

**Manager Note:** Include 3 common manager mistakes in performance reviews and how to avoid them (recency bias, halo/horn effect, vague feedback).

---

### POLICY 11: PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE POLICY

Include:
- Philosophy statement (discipline is corrective, not punitive — where applicable)
- Step 1: Verbal warning — when used, what is documented, employee acknowledgment
- Step 2: Written warning — format, HR involvement, employee response right
- Step 3: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) — see Policy 12 for detail
- Step 4: Termination — at HR's discretion, timing, final pay
- Serious violations that skip progressive steps and result in immediate termination (include a list of 8–10 examples: theft, violence, harassment, substance use on premises, etc.)
- Documentation retention requirement (3 years minimum)

**Manager Note:** Script for delivering a verbal warning. What to say, what not to say, how to close the conversation.

---

### POLICY 12: PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP) FRAMEWORK

Include:
- When a PIP is appropriate vs. when to proceed directly to termination
- PIP structure:
  * Issue description (specific behaviors, not personality)
  * Performance expectations (measurable, time-bound)
  * Support provided (training, check-ins, resources)
  * Milestones and review dates
  * Consequence if milestones not met
- PIP duration (typically 30–90 days)
- Employee rights during PIP (can they apply for internal roles? take PTO?)
- PIP sign-off requirement (employee, manager, HR)
- What "successful completion" means and what happens after

**Template:** Provide a fill-in PIP template at the end of this policy, ready to use.

---

### POLICY 13: TERMINATION PROCEDURES

Include:
- Voluntary resignation process (notice period expectation, knowledge transfer)
- Involuntary termination process (who must be present, HR involvement, documentation required)
- Final paycheck timing (state-specific for [State])
- Return of company property checklist
- System access revocation (IT steps, timing)
- Severance (if applicable — state "not guaranteed unless separately agreed in writing")
- COBRA notification obligation
- Reference policy (from inputs above)
- Exit interview (voluntary, HR conducted)

---

After all policies, output:
**DISCIPLINE DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST** — a manager checklist for each step of progressive discipline, ensuring nothing is missed before escalating.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- The PIP template is the most-used output — managers copy it directly into Google Docs
- For serious violations, be specific: "possession of controlled substances on company property" beats "drug use"
- Don't soften termination language — vague termination policies create wrongful termination risk, not less
- Add to serious violations list: "violation of confidentiality agreement" and "falsifying time records" — both are common and often missed
- California note: CA employees have additional protections — if in California, add "This handbook does not limit any rights under California Labor Code"
