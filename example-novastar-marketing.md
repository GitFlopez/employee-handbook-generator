**Company:** NovaStar Marketing  
**Industry:** Digital marketing agency  
**State:** Nevada  
**Employees:** 22 (14 full-time, 8 part-time/contractor)  
**Work model:** Hybrid — 2 days in office (Tue/Thu), 3 days remote  
**Tone:** Balanced professional  
**PTO model:** Accrual — 15 days/year (full-time), 5 days (part-time)

## SECTION 1: CORE POLICIES

### Policy 1: Code of Conduct

**Purpose:** NovaStar Marketing is built on three values: do great work, be good to each other, and protect our clients. This Code of Conduct defines what living those values looks like day-to-day.

**1.1 Professional Standards**
All employees represent NovaStar in every interaction — with clients, vendors, and each other. We expect:
- Honest, respectful communication at all times
- Meeting commitments or communicating early when you can't
- Ownership of mistakes — fix it, learn from it, move on
- Protection of client information as if it were our own

**1.2 Conflict of Interest**
Employees must disclose any outside work, investment, or relationship that could conflict with NovaStar's interests — including freelance work for current or prospective clients. Disclose to your manager in writing before starting any outside work.

**1.3 Social Media**
You own your personal social media accounts. But when you publicly reference NovaStar, clients, or colleagues, you are speaking in a professional context. Do not share client work, internal strategy, or confidential information. NovaStar does not require you to promote the company on your personal accounts.

**1.4 Confidentiality**
Client campaigns, strategies, pricing, and contact information are confidential. This extends after your employment ends. Violations may result in legal action.

**1.5 Violations**
Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. Serious violations (fraud, harassment, theft) may result in immediate termination.

*Manager Note: Code of Conduct violations should be documented immediately. If you witness a violation and don't document it, you've implicitly condoned it. Use the progressive discipline framework in Section 3.*

---

### Policy 2: Attendance & Hours

**Purpose:** Clear expectations about when and how you show up — whether in the office or at home.

**2.1 Standard Hours**
Full-time employees are expected to be available and working between **9:00 AM – 5:30 PM PT**, Monday through Friday. Core collaboration hours are **10:00 AM – 3:00 PM PT** — all team members must be reachable during this window regardless of location.

**2.2 Hybrid Schedule**
All full-time employees are in office on **Tuesdays and Thursdays**. Changes require manager approval at least 48 hours in advance.

**2.3 Tardiness**
Three or more unexcused late arrivals (15+ minutes) within a 30-day period will result in a verbal warning. Document via the attendance tracking system.

**2.4 Absence Reporting**
If you're unable to work, notify your manager via Slack **before 9:00 AM**. If you can't reach your manager, notify HR. Do not send an email and consider yourself covered — direct communication is required.

**2.5 Unexcused Absences**
Two unexcused absences in a 60-day period will trigger a verbal warning. Three will result in a written warning.

*Manager Note: Attendance issues are often a symptom. Before issuing a verbal warning, have a one-on-one to understand what's happening. Document both the conversation and any accommodation offered.*

---

### Policy 3: PTO & Vacation

**Purpose:** Rest is part of performance. Take your time off.

**3.1 Accrual Rate**
Full-time employees accrue **1.25 days per month** (15 days/year). Accrual begins on day 1. Part-time employees (20+ hrs/week) accrue **0.42 days per month** (5 days/year).

**3.2 Requesting Time Off**
Submit PTO requests in [HR software] at least **5 business days** in advance for absences of 1–3 days, and **10 business days** for 4+ consecutive days. Manager approval required.

**3.3 Rollover**
Up to **5 days** may carry into the next calendar year. PTO above 5 days is forfeited on January 1. No payout for unused PTO on resignation (Nevada does not require it unless stated in writing).

**3.4 Blackout Dates**
Q4 (October 15 – November 30) is a high-demand period. PTO requests during this window require manager and department head approval.

**3.5 Holidays**
NovaStar observes **10 federal holidays** plus the Friday after Thanksgiving. Full list posted annually in the company calendar.

**3.6 Sick vs. PTO**
NovaStar uses a combined PTO bank — sick days and vacation draw from the same balance.

*Manager Note: Encourage your team to take PTO. Burnout is a retention risk. If someone hasn't taken time off in 90+ days, check in.*

---

### Policy 4: Remote Work Policy

**Purpose:** We're a hybrid team. This policy exists so remote work actually works.

**4.1 Eligibility**
All full-time employees in non-client-facing roles are eligible for the hybrid schedule (2 in / 3 remote). New hires spend their first 30 days fully in-office.

**4.2 Home Office Requirements**
- Dedicated, private workspace during work hours
- Minimum 25 Mbps internet connection (test at fast.com)
- Company laptop used for all NovaStar work — no personal devices for client files
- Webcam and headset for video calls

**4.3 Availability Expectations**
Remote days are not flexible days. You are expected to be available and responsive during core hours (10 AM – 3 PM PT) and your full standard work hours. Response time on Slack: within 15 minutes during work hours.

**4.4 Equipment**
NovaStar provides a laptop and any necessary peripherals. Employees are responsible for the safe return of all equipment. A monitor stipend of **$150 one-time** is available — submit a receipt to HR within 30 days of hire.

**4.5 Security**
- Use the company VPN when accessing client data remotely
- Lock your screen when stepping away
- Do not work from coffee shops or public Wi-Fi without VPN

*Manager Note: Remote work is a privilege that follows performance, not a right that precedes it. If a remote employee is missing deliverables or response times, address it as a performance issue — the remote arrangement may need to be revisited.*

## SECTION 3: PERFORMANCE & DISCIPLINE (Highlights)

**Review Cycle:** Annual (January) + 90-day new hire check-in.

**Progressive Discipline:**
1. Verbal warning → documented in personnel file
2. Written warning → employee may respond in writing within 5 days
3. PIP (30–90 days, specific measurable milestones)
4. Termination

**Immediate Termination Without Progressive Steps:**
Theft, violence or threats, harassment (confirmed), falsifying records, sharing confidential client data, substance use on premises.

**PIP Example for NovaStar:**

*Employee:* Jordan Kim, Account Manager  
*Issue:* Missed 3 client deliverable deadlines in 45 days; client escalation on two accounts  
*Expectation:* Zero missed deadlines over next 60 days; proactive client communication 48 hrs before any risk  
*Support:* Weekly 1:1 with manager, access to project management training  
*Milestone 1 (Day 30):* All assigned deliverables on time; no client escalations  
*Milestone 2 (Day 60):* Performance sustained; manager assessment of client relationship health  
*Consequence if milestones not met:* Employment may be terminated
